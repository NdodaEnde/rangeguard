# RangeGuard — Pitch to Range Owner

*Use this as your talking points. Keep it conversational — you're solving his problem, not selling tech.*

---

## Opening (30 seconds)

> "I've been coming to your range and I noticed something that's costing you money every single day. Customers pay for one bucket, finish it, walk over to the short game area, fill up from the balls lying around, and walk back to the range for another free session. Some do it the other way — pay R20 for short game, then grab range balls. The sticker system doesn't stop it. Nobody's checking."

*Pause. Let him react. He knows this happens.*

---

## The Problem in Rands (1 minute)

> "Let's be conservative. Say 20 customers a day, and just 2 or 3 of them do this. That's 2 stolen buckets a day at R75 each."

| | Daily | Monthly | Annually |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stolen buckets | 2-3 | 60-90 | 730-1,095 |
| Revenue lost | R150-R225 | R4,500-R6,750 | R54,750-R82,125 |

> "That's R50,000 to R80,000 a year walking out the door. And you can't see it because nobody's counting."

---

## The Solution (2 minutes)

> "I've built a system called RangeGuard. It uses your existing cameras — the ones already installed — to track people as they move between the range and the short game area."

**How it works (keep it simple):**

> "The system watches your camera feeds and recognises people. If someone finishes at the range, walks to the short game area, spends a few minutes there, then walks back to the range — it flags it immediately. You get an alert on your phone or on a screen in the office."

**What you DON'T need to say:**
- Don't mention YOLOv8, ByteTrack, OSNet, or any tech names
- Don't explain how computer vision works
- Don't talk about Python or APIs

**What you DO say:**
> "It works with your existing cameras. No new cameras needed. No barriers, no turnstiles, nothing that changes the customer experience. It's invisible to your customers but it sees everything."

---

## The Demo (3-5 minutes)

*Open the dashboard on your laptop. The demo should already be running.*

Walk him through:

1. **Revenue Saved card** — "This number shows how much money the system has saved you today. Each time it catches someone, R75 gets added."

2. **Alert Feed** — "These are the alerts. See this one? Person #4 was at the range, walked to the short game area for 9 seconds, then came back. That's a stolen bucket."

3. **Zone path** — Point to `Driving Range → Short Game Area → Driving Range`. "This is the movement pattern. Normal customers don't do this."

4. **Movement Timeline** — Click an alert. "You can see exactly where the person went and when. If you ever need to review, it's all here."

5. **Zone Occupancy** — "You can also see in real-time how many people are at the range vs the short game area. Right now there are 3 at the range, 1 at short game."

6. **Camera Status** — "All three cameras are connected and live."

> "This is running on simulated data right now. When I connect to your cameras, it'll track your actual customers in real-time."

---

## Why He Should Care Beyond Theft (1 minute)

> "The theft detection gets your money back. But once the cameras are connected, I can also show you things like:"

- **When your busiest hours are** — so you can staff accordingly
- **How long customers actually stay** — are they getting value from their bucket?
- **Which areas are underused** — maybe the short game area needs promotion
- **After-hours security** — alerts if anyone's on the range when you're closed

> "It's like having an operations manager who never sleeps, never misses anything, and costs less than a part-time employee."

---

## The Ask (1 minute)

> "Here's what I need from you to get this running:"

1. **Access to your camera feeds** — I connect to what you already have
2. **15 minutes walking the site** — so I can map out the zones
3. **A week of testing** — I'll run it quietly, prove it works, show you the data

> "No upfront cost for the trial. I'll install it, run it for a week, and show you exactly how many incidents it catches. If the numbers don't convince you, I'll disconnect and walk away. No risk."

---

## Pricing (only if he asks)

Don't lead with pricing. Let the demo and the R50k+ annual loss number sink in first.

If he asks:

> "The setup is a one-time cost — I use your existing cameras, I just need a small processing unit that sits in your office. Then there's a monthly fee for the software, monitoring, and updates."

**Suggested pricing structure:**

| | Once-off | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| **RangeGuard Protect** | R5,000 setup | R1,500/month |
| Includes | Installation, zone mapping, calibration | Theft detection, alerts, dashboard, support |

> "At R1,500 a month, the system pays for itself if it catches just one stolen bucket every day. We've shown it catches 2-3."

*If he wants the full analytics (Layer 2):*

| | Once-off | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| **RangeGuard Pro** | R5,000 setup | R2,500/month |
| Adds | Everything in Protect + | Occupancy analytics, peak hours, session tracking, after-hours security |

**Don't discount.** If he pushes back on price, point to the annual loss number. R1,500/month vs R50,000+/year lost is not a hard equation.

---

## Objections & Responses

**"I'll just hire someone to watch."**
> "A person costs R8,000-R12,000/month, works 8 hours, takes breaks, and misses things. This runs 24/7 for R1,500 and never blinks."

**"What about privacy? Customers won't like cameras."**
> "Your cameras are already there — we're not adding new ones. We don't record faces or store personal information. The system tracks movement patterns, not identities. There are no privacy changes from what you already have."

**"How do I know it works?"**
> "Free trial. One week. I'll show you the data. If it doesn't catch anything, you owe me nothing."

**"The stickers work fine."**
> "When's the last time someone actually checked a sticker? The stickers tell people what they paid for. They don't stop anyone from ignoring it."

**"It sounds expensive."**
> "You're losing R4,500 a month minimum. The system costs R1,500 a month. That's a 3x return. And that's just on theft — the analytics alone would cost you R2,000/month from any other provider."

**"Can I just buy it outright?"**
> "The hardware is yours. The software needs updates, new detection patterns, and support — that's what the monthly covers. But I'm open to an annual deal if you want to commit — that would be R15,000/year instead of R18,000."
